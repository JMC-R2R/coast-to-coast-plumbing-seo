How AI-Overview blogs & GMB posts back the keywords

The location pages do the lead-driving; the blogs and GMB posts feed them. Blogs are written to win AI Overviews — Google's AI-generated answer at the top of results — and to pass internal-link authority to the money pages. GMB posts send fresh local-relevance signals and drive the map pack — the box that wins plumbing clicks.

What makes a blog win an AI Overview: a direct, quotable answer in the first 2–3 sentences; clear question-style H2s; short scannable lists and steps; an FAQ block with FAQ schema; and genuine local/expert detail (E-E-A-T) Google's model will cite. ⚡ Power Mode — owning AI Overviews & the LLMs

The 12 core blogs already chase AI Overviews. Power Mode is the extra gear — an additional stream of answer-first articles (3–4 a month, on top of the core 10 pages) engineered to dominate Google's AI Overview and get Coast to Coast named by the large language models — ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity and Copilot — when locals across the Brighton→Portsea corridor ask them a plumbing question. Crucially, these target conversational, long-tail and local-recommendation questions (e.g. "best plumber to unblock a drain in Mornington") that the core blogs deliberately don't — so nothing competes with itself.

⚡ Why it works
  • Answer-first format — a direct, quotable 2–3 sentence answer up top is exactly what AI Overviews and LLMs lift and cite.
  • Entity & schema depth — FAQ, HowTo and LocalBusiness schema plus clear definitions tell the models precisely what we do and where (the corridor).
  • Citable specifics — costs, steps, comparisons and genuine local detail give the models a reason to quote us over a generic national source.
  • Topical saturation — answering every question around a service makes Coast to Coast the obvious authority the models default to.
