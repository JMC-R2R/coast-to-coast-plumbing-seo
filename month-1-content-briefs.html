Title (H1)Blocked Drains: Is It the Tenant's or Landlord's Responsibility? (VIC)
MetaWho pays for a blocked drain in a Victorian rental — tenant or landlord? The general rules, when each is liable, and what to do next. A Coast to Coast guide.
Answer-first snippet: In Victoria, the landlord is generally responsible for fixing blocked drains caused by fair wear and tear, tree roots or ageing pipes, as part of keeping the property in good repair. The tenant is usually responsible only if they caused the blockage — for example by flushing wipes, sanitary items or grease. Either way, report a blocked drain to the property manager promptly.
